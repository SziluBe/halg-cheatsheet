\wde{1.8.1 Image \& Kernel} The image of a linear map $f : V \to W$ is the subspace $\im(f) = f(V) \subseteq W$. The preimage of the zero vector is the subspace denoted $\ker(f) = f^{-1}(0) = \{v \in V | f(v)=0\}$.
\wl{1.8.2 Injectivity} $f$ is injective iff. the kernel is 0. $f$ is surjective if $\im(f) = W$. For $\dim V < \infty$, $f$ is surjective if  $\dim(\im(f)) = \dim W$.
\wt{1.8.4 Rank-Nullity} $\dim V = \dim(\ker f) + \dim(\im f) = \text{nullity}(f) + \text{rank}(f)$
